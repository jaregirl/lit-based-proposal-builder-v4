About & Sources

App Attribution

Lit-Based Proposal Builder was developed by J. Arawiran with assistance from OpenAI Codex, a GPT-5-based coding assistant (June 2026).

This app is a guided academic thinking tool designed to support the development of research proposals. It assists users in organizing ideas, identifying research problems, aligning research questions with methods, and drafting proposal components. It does not replace adviser feedback, instructor requirements, department policies, institutional guidelines, or official ethics review processes.

Data Privacy and Local Storage

The app saves draft work locally in the user's own browser storage. The developer, instructor, and app do not receive, collect, or store the student's answers through this tool. Draft data is not transmitted to a cloud server by the app. Students should still use the Download Backup feature before changing devices, clearing browser data, or updating the app.

Conceptual and Educational Foundations

The design of this proposal builder draws upon established literature in research methodology, educational research, research ethics, and reflective inquiry. These sources informed the structure and guidance provided by the app. They are not automatically part of a student's proposal references unless they are directly consulted and cited by the student.

Research Methodology Foundations

The proposal-development workflow is informed by widely used approaches to research design and proposal writing, particularly the work of:

  • Creswell, J. W., & Creswell, J. D. on qualitative, quantitative, and mixed methods research design.
  • Cohen, L., Manion, L., & Morrison, K. on educational research methods and research ethics.

These works informed the app's guidance on problem formulation, purpose statements, research questions, methodological alignment, and proposal structure.

Research Ethics Foundations

The ethical guidance embedded in the app is informed by:

  • Philippine Health Research Ethics Board (PHREB) National Ethical Guidelines for Health and Health-Related Research (2022).
  • American Educational Research Association (AERA) Code of Ethics.
  • British Educational Research Association (BERA) Ethical Guidelines.
  • Ethical principles discussed in Creswell and Creswell and in Cohen, Manion, and Morrison.

These sources informed prompts related to informed consent, confidentiality, anonymity, voluntary participation, potential risks and benefits, vulnerable participants, and conflicts of interest.

Degree-Level Readiness and PQF Reference

The app can be used for SHS/basic education research, bachelor's work, master's work, and doctoral work. For SHS/basic education, the app uses PQF Level 3 as a formative reference, looking for structured research tasks, emerging independence, accountability for outputs, and feasible application of foundational research knowledge. For bachelor's, master's, and doctoral work, the app uses the revised Philippine Qualifications Framework domains as a formative guide for degree-level readiness: Responsibility and Autonomy, Knowledge and Understanding, and Skills and Application.

PQF reference used: PQF-NCC Resolution 2026-02. This app provides formative guidance only and does not certify PQF compliance. Verify the latest PQF issuances through official PQF-NCC, DepEd, CHED, TESDA, PRC, or institutional sources.

Research Thinking Philosophy

The app reflects the view that research is a process of refining explanations through evidence, discussion, and revision rather than defending fixed ideas. This perspective is reflected in the Research Thinking Guidelines used in the course and in the design of the A1-A4 proposal-writing scaffolds developed by J. Arawiran.

The app also reflects the perspective articulated by Schwartz, M. A. (2008), The Importance of Stupidity in Scientific Research. This work influenced the treatment of uncertainty, revision, intellectual risk-taking, and the understanding that confusion and incomplete knowledge are normal parts of the research process.
